Illinois Resources

Explore a variety of programs and services available specifically for Illinois residents.

Illinois Housing Resources

  • Illinois Department of Human Services: Provides information on homeless prevention and support services for Illinois residents.
  • Illinois Housing Development Authority: Offers resources for affordable rental housing throughout Illinois.
  • DCEO Housing Assistance: Provides various housing assistance programs through the Illinois Department of Commerce and Economic Opportunity.
  • Illinois Housing Help: Offers resources for rental assistance, mortgage assistance, and homelessness prevention.

Illinois Utility Assistance

  • DCEO Utility Bill Assistance: Information on Illinois programs that help with utility bills through the Department of Commerce and Economic Opportunity.
  • How to Apply for Utility Assistance: Step-by-step guidance on applying for utility bill assistance in Illinois.
  • Home Weatherization & Community Action Agencies: Resources for home weatherization assistance and local community action agencies that can help.
  • CEDA Gas and Electric Assistance: The Community and Economic Development Association offers help with gas and electric bills for eligible residents.
  • Help Illinois Families: Provides information about various assistance programs for Illinois families, including utility assistance.
  • Catholic Charities: Offers various assistance programs including utility bill help for those in need.
  • USA.gov Help with Energy Bills: Federal resources for energy bill assistance that Illinois residents can access.

Illinois Food & Nutrition Resources

  • Illinois DHS – Apply for Benefits: Apply for SNAP, medical, and cash assistance through the Department of Human Services.
  • ABE Illinois (Application for Benefits Eligibility): Use this online portal to apply for SNAP (food stamps), medical, and other public benefits.
  • Illinois.gov – Apply for SNAP and Medical Assistance: Direct access to state services for food and healthcare benefits.
  • Northern Illinois Food Bank – SNAP Help: Offers help applying for SNAP benefits and information on local food resources.
  • We Got You Illinois – SNAP Support: A campaign helping individuals understand and enroll in SNAP food benefits.
  • Illinois Department on Aging – Nutrition Programs: Statewide senior nutrition services including home-delivered and group meals.
  • Illinois DHS – Food Assistance Programs: Overview of food-related programs including SNAP and child nutrition.
  • Illinois DHS – Nutrition Programs for Women, Infants, and Children (WIC): Resources for WIC services across the state for pregnant women, infants, and children under five.
  • Illinois DHS – Emergency Food Program: Provides emergency food support through local food pantries and community agencies.

Illinois Dental Resources

Explore dental care options and training-based clinics available across Illinois.

  • Find a Dental Clinic (ISDS): A listing of clinics for people with limited financial ability provided by the Illinois State Dental Society.
  • Illinois' Federally Qualified Health Centers: These centers are for people who are low income, uninsured, or are undocumented residents.
  • University of Illinois - Chicago: Provides urgent dental care and services. Call: 312-996-8636
  • Southern Illinois University - Alton: Offers dental services through their educational programs. Call: 618-474-7000
  • Midwestern University - Downers Grove: Provides dental care through their teaching clinics. Call: 630-743-4500

Illinois Senior Resources

Explore senior-focused services including benefits, caregiving support, and local aging resources.

  • Illinois DHS – Senior Services: Offers community-based services for seniors including caregiver support and elder abuse prevention.
  • DHS Adult Protective Services: Protects older adults and adults with disabilities from abuse, neglect, and exploitation.
  • Illinois Department on Aging Helpline: Call 1-800-252-8966 for information on senior programs, benefits, and support services.
  • Elderwerks Directory: Search senior housing, home care, and supportive services across Illinois.
  • Elderwerks Partner Resources: Explore connections to trusted service providers for older adults in Illinois.
  • Illinois State & Federal Resource Guide for Older Adults: Comprehensive PDF guide to programs and services for seniors statewide.

Illinois Transportation Resources

Explore transportation options for seniors, people with disabilities, and Medicaid recipients across Illinois.

  • Pace ADA Paratransit Services: Provides accessible transportation for riders with disabilities in suburban Chicago, including Ride Free and Reduced Fare programs.
  • Pace ADA Certification: Learn how to get certified for paratransit services and schedule accessible rides through Pace.
  • Regional Transportation Authority (RTA): Offers reduced fare and Ride Free programs for seniors and riders with disabilities in the Chicago area.
  • Illinois Senior Ride Free Program: Allows eligible seniors and people with disabilities to ride public transportation free of charge statewide.
  • IDHS Transportation Programs: Provides non-emergency transportation services for medical visits and other essential appointments.
  • Illinois HFS – Non-Emergency Medical Transportation: Offers transport services for eligible Medicaid recipients traveling to healthcare appointments.
  • Access Living – Chicago Transit Resources: Detailed guides on accessible transit services for people with disabilities in the Chicagoland area.
  • City of Chicago MOPD – Accessible Transportation: Resources on accessible taxis, public transit, and paratransit for people with disabilities.

Chicago / Cook County Resources

Explore support services available to residents of Chicago and Cook County, including housing, legal aid, crisis response, and public assistance.

  • City of Chicago – Resident Resources: Comprehensive guide to housing, food, healthcare, and financial assistance programs.
  • 211 Metro Chicago: Connects residents to essential health and social services, including shelter, food, and mental health support.
  • DFSS Community Service Centers: Six centers offering in-person benefit assessments, shelter, food, clothing, and job training. Open Monday–Friday, 9am–5pm.
  • Eviction Legal Assistance: Contact Cook County Legal Aid for Housing and Debt at 855-956-5763 for support with eviction and housing debt.
  • Homelessness Prevention Call Center: Call 312-744-5000 for housing instability and emergency shelter resources.
  • Chicago Senior Services Hotline: Call 312-744-4016, Mon–Fri, 8:30am–4:30pm, for services and support tailored to older adults.
  • Illinois Domestic Violence Helpline: Call 1-877-863-6338 for 24/7 confidential support and referrals.
  • NAMI Mental Health Helpline: Contact 1-800-950-NAMI, Mon–Fri, 9am–5pm, for mental health information and resources.
  • Illinois Opioid and Substance Use Helpline: Call 1-833-2FINDHELP for treatment referrals and recovery support.
  • National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: Call or text 988, or chat online 24/7 for immediate crisis support.
